Creative Engineer

Hello and welcome to my little corner of the internet, I am a software engineer, gamer, artist, plant lover and much more, based in Gothenburg, Sweden. I have over 10 years of experience in startups, consulting and large enterprises across Scotland, Germany and Sweden.

When I applied for the computer science program at the University of Gothenburg in spring of 2010 I was torn, because at the same time I also applied for an acting school a bit outside the city. Two completely different paths in life, one creative path and one rational (in a dual sense). As you might understand, I chose the latter.

Luckily, the route we take professionally has no real imprint on what we really are. That's why I call myself a Creative Engineer, and that's something I try to show with this website which I wrote during my parental leave in 2025 in native JavaScript (consider that a personal opinion about modern web development).

Oted Online

My online alias started as Detox in the game World of Warcraft around 2005. I later thought it was too cringe and reversed the word it into Xoted, where the X went in the end I cant fully remember but since then it has been Oted and this website is thus me online! . It serves as a semi professional portfolio but is also a host for private purposes such as selfhosting, personal server playground and a rather sophisticated media management cloud Arr!

The algorithm running in the canvas is similar to Conway's Game of Life with some personal adjustments. The architecture is event-driven where one tile can trigger its neighbours limited by a declining strength of the color it contains. Check it out on GitHub!

Disclaimer: oted.online is made entirely without AI! Did you know that in May 2025 the amount of content created on the internet by AI surpassed content made by us. When an ever increasing amount of what we consume online is just a generic sum over all created in the past, what does it do to the internet and what we consume? When the data fed to the models is also increasingly made by AI? Where does poetry live then?

Inspirations

Below are a few visionaries that have shaped me to the man I am today, they all walked their own path in life and strived towards seeing truthfully on the world and society.

Buckminster Fuller
Mr Fuller dedicated his entire life for the better of humanity and believed that by continuously doing more with less with science and technology, we can free society from scarcity and there need not be any suffering.

Alan Watts
Although criticized by the Zen community I have nothing but gratitude to Alan for introducing Zen to me. His lectures in the Out of your mind series is something I return to over and over and it has brought me back to the cushion and the wall countless times.

Jacque Fresco
Another man who walked his own path in life (yes it's a theme here). He envisioned a society free from the constraints of the monetary system. Instead he argued it's possible to create a resource based economy which would be based on the latest scientific and technological knowledge. Think about it like the principles of a library but for everything we need in life. Because will we ever have peace as long as some have nothing while others have everything? Is it really human nature to fight? Or do we fight simply because the resources are not distributed properly?

Kjell Höglund
Men för mig är ju hela showen gåtfull. Många människor tycker inte det är något konstigt. Stjärnor på himmelen, atomer och det växer träd å de…. Men jag liksom ropar mot himmelen och undrar vad in i helvete är det för nånting va? Och där nånstans tror jag skillnaden ligger, att man antingen tar livet för givet eller bara tycker det är fullständigt obegripligt.
Kjell is not just a great musician, but a poet and a philosopher. He delivers his ideas about the world with a perfect mix of humor, thought and catchiness. I keep finding new meaning in his work still today and his music has inspired me to think for myself since childhood.

Journey

A timeline of significance
2024
Parenthood

Mid November 2024 my firstborn son came to be and he has changed my world forever. As other parents know perspectives changes and life takes on a new meaning and I am so lucky to experience this unfolding of consciousness and our little family.

2021
All roads lead home

Borta bra men hemma bäst is a saying in Swedish and it sums up the decision to move back to Gothenburg. I wanted to be close to my family and maybe one day get one of my own, perhaps even a garden for Cashew and all my plants!

Still working with GRID on a remote basis I took on the role as Staff Engineer which has widened my role significantly and I truly enjoy the challenges that it brings.

2016
Liebe Berlin

I have so much love for the city of Berlin and the time I spent there and it is hard to describe this nostalgic period. Berlin is a thriving place both in and out of tech and the people and the experiences I had here grew me into the man I am today.

Soon after the move I switched to a new job in a larger company in the dating industry. I got to work with some very talented people and learned Golang inside and out. We also dealt with a lot of purchased software which forced me to learn many other technologies.

After a few years I needed a break and took some time unemployed before I focused my attention again on the start-up sector and found a job at GRID Esports. GRID is a perfect match for me not only because I get to work with my favorite game Dota 2, but also because the tech stack is super interesting, I especially enjoy real time data processing challenges using Kafka.

I also got Cashew! She is the cutest, grumpiest and most adorable little thing ever created. Love you little rat <3

2014
Hello Scotland!

After my degree I spread my applications wide across Europe with a focus on start-ups. I was very close ending up in London, but a small fashion company contacted me from Edinburgh and I got my first job as a Backend Engineer working mostly in NodeJS. I spent the following years in the beautiful city of Edinburgh, working and making connections with many important people in my life. I also started a Meetup to play boardgames, it is still the largest board game meetup in the city!

2012
Consultancy

My first experience within the tech industry was during the last years of my CS program and it was naturally an intense period with both school and work. It however gave me a great advantage when I applied for work after, since most new grads have no work experience when applying for their first job.

2010
University

I finally decided to do something more useful and applied late to the Computer Science program at the University of Gothenburg and got in! It's a good program with relevant but hard courses. I still remember the first course we had was functional programming with Haskell and by the end of the semester the class had literally halved in size. This was an ongoing trend and only a handful of students made it through the entire program. That I was one of them is something that makes me proud to this day.

2009
Blunder years

The years prior to University I was living peak life according to my 20 year old self. I moved here and there in Europe and played Musical Saw in bars and for good money in the street. Youtube might or might not still have glimpses of this life.

Projects

Over the years I have completed several projects that I'm proud of. Some of them had thousands of users and most of them fell into the void due to lack of time and maintenance from my part.
[2025] Oted Online
This website is my latest project and serves as a semi professional portfolio but also a personal hub for content and media managments. The algorithm running in the canvas was especially fun to implement where I could challenge myself with both optimizing the canvas drawing and write in native Javascript without bloated frameworks.
[2023] What to Watch
This webapp collected new movies and TV Shows and allowed users to easily filter and sort based of preferences! It also sent emails every Friday with recommendations to users, unfortionally the database with all of the emails was lost, I abandoned the projects due to lack of time when my son was born.
[2017] Get some Internet
GSI was a pet project of mine mostly during the years in Scotland. It was a great entertainment website with a rather early adoption of infinite scroll of recent popular videos, gifs and images.
[2017] Mandelbrot Calc
A small project I did to both learn more about both the Mandelbrot set and Rust, but also to visually understand what happens with the set as Z changes. Perhaps one day I should improve the canvas display since it's a bit grainy on deeper zooms.
[2015] Youdify
This project was a collaborative YouTube playlist, people could join the same "room" and queue videos over websockets at parties and gatherings. It was never marketed and thus only used by me and my friends. Still a great product and a great learning experience.
[2014] Swiftsubs
My first ever project on the web was actually the most successful one with thousands of daily users. It was a simple search engine for subtitles online, you could drag and drop a video and the engine would try to locate the exact match via web crawlers. Unfortunately the payment for the domain failed one year and somebody bought it up before I could.
